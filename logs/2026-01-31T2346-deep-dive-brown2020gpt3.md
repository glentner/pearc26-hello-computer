## Key Findings

- **Scale**: 175B parameters (100x GPT-2, 10x any prior non-sparse LM)
- **Core Innovation**: Few-shot learning / in-context learning—model adapts to tasks without weight updates
- **Emergent Behavior**: In-context learning capability scales with model size
- **Training**: ~300B tokens, 3.14 × 10²³ FLOPs, trained on V100 GPU cluster
- **Results**: SOTA on TriviaQA (71.2%), competitive on SuperGLUE, surprising arithmetic abilities
- **Impact**: Established "foundation model" paradigm, led to ChatGPT revolution

## Relevance to Our Paper

GPT-3 is the critical bridge between the Transformer architecture (2017) and the practical AI
revolution (2022+). It demonstrated that scale produces emergent capabilities, establishing:

1. The compute-hungry nature of frontier AI (HPC relevance)
2. The foundation model paradigm (one model, many tasks)
3. The path to agentic AI (flexible task adaptation at inference)

This paper is why our "HPC in the Agentic Era" thesis matters—without scale, there would be
no emergent capabilities, no ChatGPT, no agentic tools.
